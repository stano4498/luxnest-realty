Buying Property

The property buying process in Kenya involves several key steps:

  • Property Search: Identify and view properties that meet your criteria
  • Due Diligence: Conduct property searches at the Ministry of Lands
  • Negotiation: Agree on price and terms with the seller
  • Sale Agreement: Sign a sale agreement and pay deposit (usually 10%)
  • Financing: Secure mortgage approval if needed
  • Transfer: Complete the transfer of ownership at the Ministry of Lands
  • Registration: Register the property in your name

The entire process typically takes 3-6 months depending on financing and legal requirements.

The deposit requirements vary depending on your financing method:

  • Bank Mortgage: Typically 20-30% of the property value
  • SACCO Loans: Usually 10-20% deposit required
  • Developer Financing: Can be as low as 5-10%
  • Cash Purchase: No deposit required, but you pay full amount

For a property worth KES 10 million, you'd typically need KES 2-3 million as deposit for a bank mortgage.

You'll need the following documents:

  • National ID or Passport (for foreigners)
  • KRA PIN Certificate
  • Bank statements (last 6 months)
  • Salary certificate or business registration
  • Marriage certificate (if married)
  • Mortgage pre-approval letter (if applicable)

For the property: Title deed, survey map, rates clearance certificate, and building plan approvals.

  • Freehold Land: Only Kenyan citizens can own freehold land

Beyond the property price, budget for these additional costs:

  • Stamp Duty: 4% of property value
  • Legal Fees: 1-2% of property value
  • Valuation Fees: KES 15,000 - 50,000
  • Survey Fees: KES 20,000 - 100,000
  • Registration Fees: KES 1,000 - 10,000
  • Agent Commission: 2-3% (usually paid by seller)

Total additional costs typically range from 6-8% of the property value.

Selling Property

Property valuation considers several factors:

  • Location: Proximity to amenities, schools, and transport
  • Property Size: Land size and built-up area
  • Property Condition: Age, maintenance, and renovations
  • Market Comparables: Recent sales of similar properties
  • Market Conditions: Current supply and demand

We offer free property valuations to help you set the right price for quick sale.

Sale timeframes vary by location and property type:

  • Prime Locations (Nairobi, Mombasa): 3-6 months
  • Suburban Areas: 6-12 months
  • Rural/Remote Areas: 12+ months
  • Commercial Properties: 6-18 months

Factors affecting sale speed include pricing, marketing, property condition, and market demand.

Essential documents for selling include:

  • Original Title Deed
  • Survey Map/Site Plan
  • Rates Clearance Certificate
  • Land Rent Clearance (for leasehold)
  • Building Plan Approvals
  • Occupancy Certificate
  • National ID/Passport
  • KRA PIN Certificate

Our team helps verify all documents are in order before listing your property.

Capital gains tax applies in certain situations:

  • Primary Residence: No capital gains tax if it's your main home
  • Investment Property: 5% tax on gains for residents, 10% for non-residents
  • Commercial Property: Subject to capital gains tax
  • Holding Period: Properties held over 3 years may qualify for exemptions

Consult with our tax advisors for specific guidance on your situation.

Financing & Loans

Current mortgage rates vary by lender and loan type:

  • Commercial Banks: 12.5% - 16% per annum
  • SACCOs: 10% - 14% per annum
  • Microfinance: 15% - 20% per annum
  • Government Schemes: 7% - 10% per annum (limited availability)

Rates depend on your credit score, income, and loan-to-value ratio. Fixed rates are typically 1-2% higher than variable rates.

Loan amount depends on several factors:

  • Income Multiple: Usually 3-4 times your annual gross income
  • Debt-to-Income Ratio: Total monthly debts shouldn't exceed 40% of income
  • Loan-to-Value: Maximum 80-90% of property value
  • Age Factor: Loan term + age shouldn't exceed 65-70 years

Example: With monthly income of KES 200,000, you could qualify for a loan of KES 8-12 million.

Credit requirements vary by lender:

  • Excellent (750+): Best rates and terms available
  • Good (650-749): Standard rates, good approval chances
  • Fair (550-649): Higher rates, may need larger deposit
  • Poor (Below 550): Difficult to qualify, consider improving credit first

Check your CRB report before applying. We can help you improve your credit score if needed.

  • Business Owners: 2-3 years of audited accounts and tax returns
  • Freelancers: Bank statements showing consistent deposits
  • Commission-based: Average income over 2-3 years
  • Multiple Income Sources: Document all income streams

Some lenders specialize in self-employed mortgages with more flexible criteria.

Mortgage insurance protects the lender if you default:

  • When Required: Usually when deposit is less than 20%
  • Cost: 0.5% - 1.5% of loan amount annually
  • Duration: Until you have 20% equity in the property
  • Benefit: Allows you to buy with smaller deposit

Consider life insurance as well to protect your family if something happens to you.

Legal Matters

Understanding the key differences:

  • Freehold: You own the land and property indefinitely
  • Leasehold: You lease the land for a fixed period (usually 99 years)
  • Ownership Rights: Freehold gives full ownership, leasehold has restrictions
  • Ground Rent: Leasehold properties pay annual ground rent
  • Renewal: Leasehold can be renewed, but terms may change

Most urban properties in Kenya are leasehold, especially in Nairobi and Mombasa.

Follow these verification steps:

  • Ministry of Lands Search: Conduct an official search at the registry
  • Physical Inspection: Visit the property and verify boundaries
  • Survey Map: Ensure the survey map matches the physical property
  • Rates Clearance: Check that property taxes are up to date
  • Legal Review: Have a qualified lawyer review all documents

Never buy property without conducting proper due diligence. We provide comprehensive verification services.

Property disputes can be resolved through:

  • Mediation: Informal resolution with neutral mediator
  • Arbitration: Binding decision by arbitrator
  • Land Tribunal: Specialized court for land matters
  • High Court: For complex or high-value disputes

Prevention is better than cure - always ensure proper due diligence before purchase.

While not legally required, a lawyer is highly recommended for:

  • Document Review: Ensuring all paperwork is correct
  • Due Diligence: Conducting proper property searches
  • Contract Negotiation: Protecting your interests
  • Transfer Process: Handling the legal transfer
  • Risk Mitigation: Identifying potential legal issues

Legal fees are typically 1-2% of property value - a small price for peace of mind.

Investment

Rental yields vary by location and property type:

  • Nairobi CBD: 6-8% gross yield
  • Nairobi Suburbs: 4-6% gross yield
  • Mombasa: 5-7% gross yield
  • Kisumu/Nakuru: 7-10% gross yield
  • Commercial Properties: 8-12% gross yield

A net yield of 4-6% is considered good after expenses. Factor in vacancy rates and maintenance costs.

Consider your investment goals:

  • Rental Income: Focus on high-yield areas, established neighborhoods
  • Capital Growth: Look for developing areas, infrastructure projects
  • Balanced Approach: Seek properties offering both income and growth
  • Time Horizon: Short-term investors prefer income, long-term prefer growth

The best strategy often combines both - steady rental income with long-term appreciation.

Rental income tax considerations:

  • Residential Rental: 10% withholding tax on gross rent
  • Commercial Rental: Subject to normal income tax rates
  • Deductions: Maintenance, repairs, insurance, management fees
  • Depreciation: Can claim depreciation on buildings
  • VAT: May apply to commercial rentals over KES 5 million annually

Keep detailed records and consult with a qualified tax advisor for optimization strategies.

Investment property financing options:

  • Investment Mortgages: Higher rates than residential mortgages
  • Cash Purchase: Better negotiating power, immediate rental income
  • Joint Ventures: Partner with other investors
  • Developer Financing: Flexible payment terms for new developments
  • REIT Investment: Indirect property investment through listed REITs

Investment mortgages typically require 30-40% deposit and have higher interest rates.

General Questions

Look for these qualities in a real estate agent:

  • Experience: Track record in your target area
  • Credentials: Licensed and registered with professional bodies
  • Local Knowledge: Deep understanding of the local market
  • Communication: Responsive and keeps you informed
  • References: Positive reviews from past clients
  • Marketing: Strong online presence and marketing strategy

Interview multiple agents and choose one who understands your needs and communicates well.

Timing considerations for property purchase:

  • Market Cycles: Buy during market downturns for better prices
  • Personal Readiness: When you have stable income and deposit
  • Interest Rates: Lower rates mean cheaper borrowing
  • Seasonal Factors: More inventory available in certain months
  • Economic Conditions: Political stability affects property prices

The best time is when you're financially ready and find the right property at a fair price.

COVID-19 impacts on the property market:

  • Price Adjustments: Some softening in high-end segments
  • Rental Market: Increased demand for larger homes, reduced office space
  • Financing: Tighter lending criteria from banks
  • Construction: Delays in new developments
  • Digital Adoption: More virtual viewings and online transactions

The market is recovering with pent-up demand and government stimulus measures.

Key things to check during property viewings:

  • Structural Issues: Cracks, water damage, foundation problems
  • Utilities: Water pressure, electrical systems, internet connectivity
  • Security: Neighborhood safety, security features
  • Amenities: Proximity to schools, hospitals, shopping
  • Transport: Access to public transport, traffic patterns
  • Future Development: Planned infrastructure or developments nearby

Visit at different times of day to get a complete picture. Don't rush - take your time to evaluate.

Kenya's property market compared to regional peers:

  • South Africa: Generally more expensive, mature market
  • Nigeria: Lagos comparable to Nairobi, but more volatile
  • Ghana: Accra slightly cheaper than Nairobi
  • Tanzania: Dar es Salaam generally cheaper than Nairobi
  • Uganda: Kampala significantly cheaper than Nairobi

Kenya offers good value with stable governance, growing economy, and established legal framework.
